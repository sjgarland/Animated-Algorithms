Algorithms get things done. Young children learn algorithms for brushing their teeth and tying their shoes. In school, they learn mathematical algorithms for

  • adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing multi-digit decimal numbers,
  • finding the roots of a quadratic equation using the quadratic formula,
  • finding the length of the hypotenuse of a right triangle using the Pythagorean theorem, and
  • finding final grades in a course by computing weighted averages of exam and homework scores and then scaling the results.

Algorithms underly important applications of computing. They are used to transmit data efficiently, reliably, and securely over the internet, to recognize speech and images, to control robots, and much more.

In general, an algorithm is a set of rules or instructions for solving a class of problems or for performing a computation. Just as it is easier to learn how to tie shoe laces by seeing it done rather than by reading rules in a book, it is easier to learn how algorithms work by seeing them in action—not by just reading computer code.

This website makes prominent use of animations to show how algorithms work.
